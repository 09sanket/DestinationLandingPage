Madhya Pradesh, known as the heart of India, is home to some of the country's most magnificent historical landmarks. From the intricately carved temples of Khajuraho to the ancient Buddhist site of Sanchi Stupa, the state showcases a rich cultural legacy.

Sanchi Stupa

The Great Stupa at Sanchi was originally commissioned by Emperor Ashoka in the 3rd century BCE. It is one of the oldest stone structures in India and an important monument of Buddhist architecture. The site features several stupas, temples, and monasteries.

Khajuraho Group of Monuments

The temples at Khajuraho were built during the Chandella dynasty, which reached its apogee between 950 and 1050. Only about 20 temples remain; they fall into three distinct groups and belong to two different religions – Hinduism and Jainism.
